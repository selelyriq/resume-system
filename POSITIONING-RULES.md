# Positioning Rules & Ethics Guardrails

## Core Principles

This system is built on **complete honesty and integrity**. Positioning is about emphasis and framing, not fabrication.

### What is Positioning

- ✅ Reordering bullets to lead with the most relevant work
- ✅ Emphasizing the aspects of a project that align with the target role
- ✅ Framing the same work from different angles (platform vs. automation vs. AI infra)
- ✅ Highlighting experiences that match the interview focus
- ✅ Being strategic about which stories to tell

### What is NOT Positioning

- ❌ Fabricating experiences or projects
- ❌ Exaggerating impact metrics
- ❌ Claiming ownership of work done by others
- ❌ Adding skills not actually used or learned
- ❌ Misleading about timeline or responsibility level

## Specific Guardrails

### Metrics & Impact
- **Only cite metrics you directly influenced.** The 3x deployment speed? You worked on it and measured it. The $80M/day? That's the system's throughput — you helped migrate it, not generate it.
- **Be specific about your role.** "Led" vs. "contributed to" vs. "participated in" are materially different.
- **If unsure, qualify it.** "Helped reduce deployment failures by 60%" is more honest than implying sole responsibility.

### Skill Claims
- **Only claim skills you've actively used.** Tinkered with something once is "familiar with," not "experienced in."
- **Be prepared to go deep on anything you list.** If it's on the resume, you should be able to discuss it technically.

### Work Ownership
- **Credit your team.** Infrastructure work happens because teams coordinate. You were a critical contributor — own that, without erasing others.
- **Don't claim collaborative work as solo.** The $80M cutover succeeded because of your preparation AND team execution.

### Stories & Framing
- **Stories must be technically truthful.** You can frame Terraform state isolation as a platform story, automation story, or AI-infra story — all true. You can't frame it as inventing something novel that doesn't exist.
- **Adapt, don't invent.** Each story is a real experience; different positionings emphasize different aspects.
- **Be ready to walk through the details.** If you tell a story in an interview, you should be able to explain exactly how you did it.

## Red Lines

1. **Do not fabricate projects.** Never list work you didn't do.
2. **Do not exaggerate timelines.** 3 months is 3 months.
3. **Do not claim skills you don't have.** If you can't talk about it technically, don't list it.
4. **Do not mislead about collaboration.** Own your role without diminishing your team's.
5. **Do not commit fraud.** Employers verify. Fabrications get discovered.

## Honest Framing Example

**The work**: Built Terraform modules for AWS networking

**Platform Engineering frame**: "Designed reusable, composable Terraform modules that standardize AWS networking, enabling teams to safely deploy infrastructure without deep cloud expertise."

**Infra Automation frame**: "Implemented Terraform modules integrated into GitHub Actions CI/CD pipelines, enabling automated, validated network deployments with plan/apply gating."

**AI/ML Infra frame**: "Built modular Terraform foundations for AWS networking and IAM — the infrastructure layer that ML workloads depend on for consistent, isolated environments across accounts."

All three are true. You emphasize different outcomes of the same work.

## When in Doubt

- Would I be comfortable discussing this in detail in an interview?
- If the interviewer pushes on this claim, do I have evidence?
- Does this feel right, or am I rationalizing?

If you're rationalizing, it's probably not okay.

## Career Trajectory

**Platform Engineering → AI Infrastructure → Artifact-based Systems**

Build toward this with real skills and real experience. The positioning system is a tool for communicating what you've already done, not a shortcut to claim what you haven't.
