# Positioning Rules & Ethics Guardrails

## Core Principles

This resume system is built on a foundation of **complete honesty and integrity**. Positioning is about emphasis and framing, not fabrication.

### What is Positioning?

- ✅ Reordering bullets to lead with relevant work
- ✅ Emphasizing aspects of a project that align with the target role
- ✅ Framing the same work from different angles (platform vs. automation vs. security)
- ✅ Highlighting experiences that match the interview focus
- ✅ Being strategic about what stories to tell

### What is NOT Positioning

- ❌ Fabricating experiences or projects
- ❌ Exaggerating impact metrics ("3x improvement" when it was 30%)
- ❌ Claiming ownership of work done by colleagues
- ❌ Adding technical skills not actually used or learned
- ❌ Misleading about timeline or responsibility

## Specific Guardrails

### Metrics & Impact
- **Only cite metrics you directly influenced**. The 3x deployment speed improvement? That's real—you worked on it and measured it. The $80M/day cutover? That's the system's throughput, which you helped migrate, not your personal impact.
- **Be specific about your role**. "Led implementation" vs. "contributed to" vs. "participated in" are different.
- **If unsure, qualify it**. "Helped reduce deployment failures by 60%" is more honest than claiming sole responsibility.

### Skill Claims
- **Only claim skills you've actively used in production**. If you've tinkered with Prometheus but never deployed it in production, that's "familiar with" not "expert in."
- **Add caveats for technologies you've touched but not mastered**. "Experience with" vs. "expertise in" matter.

### Work Ownership
- **Credit your team**. Infrastructure migrations happen because teams coordinate. You were a critical part, not the sole owner.
- **Don't claim work that was collaborative**. If the $80M cutover succeeded because of your planning AND your team's execution, say so.

### Stories & Framing
- **Stories must be technically truthful**. You can frame the Terraform state isolation work as platform engineering, DevOps, or security—all true. You can't frame it as "invented a novel consensus algorithm."
- **Adapt, don't invent**. Each story is a real experience; different positionings just emphasize different aspects.
- **Be prepared to discuss details**. If you tell a story in an interview, you should be able to walk through exactly how you did it.

## Red Lines (Never Cross)

1. **Do not fabricate projects**. Never list a project you didn't work on.
2. **Do not exaggerate timelines**. If you led something for 3 months, don't say it was 6 months.
3. **Do not claim skills you don't have**. "Expert Kubernetes operator" when you've only deployed once is dangerous.
4. **Do not mislead about collaboration**. Own your role without diminishing your team's.
5. **Do not commit fraud**. If a future employer fact-checks (and many do), any fabrication will be discovered.

## Positioning Examples: The Honest Way

### Same Project, Three Honest Frames

**The work**: Built Terraform modules for AWS networking

**Platform frame**: "Designed reusable, composable Terraform modules that standardize AWS networking, enabling teams to safely deploy infrastructure without deep cloud expertise."

**Automation frame**: "Implemented Terraform modules integrated into GitHub Actions CI/CD pipeline, enabling automated, validated network deployments."

**Security frame**: "Built Terraform modules enforcing least-privilege network isolation, preventing lateral movement and reducing blast radius."

**All three are true**. You emphasize different aspects of the same work.

### Honest Impact Framing

**The work**: Helped reduce deployment times (it was a team effort)

❌ **Dishonest**: "Reduced deployment speed by 3x" (implies sole responsibility)

✅ **Honest**: "Contributed to 3x deployment speed improvement by implementing Kubernetes orchestration and GitHub Actions CI/CD"

## When in Doubt

- **Ask yourself**: Would I be comfortable discussing this in detail in an interview?
- **Apply the reverse test**: If the interviewer calls me on this claim, do I have proof?
- **Check with your instinct**: Does this feel right, or am I rationalizing?

If you're rationalizing, it's probably not okay.

## Updating This Document

As your career evolves, this document should evolve. New experiences, new skills, new stories. But the principle remains: **full integrity, strategic emphasis**.
